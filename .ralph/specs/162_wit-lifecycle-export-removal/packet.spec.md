# Packet Contract: 162_wit-lifecycle-export-removal

## Goal

Delete the `on-print-start` / `on-print-end` WIT exports and every artifact that mirrors them — SDK trait methods, macro glue and shims, `WORLD_LIFECYCLE_EXPORTS` and its self-referential guard test, the `pnp_cli module new` scaffold — renaming the surviving per-call constructor to `from_config`, and make a stale `pnp_cli` binary fail loudly instead of silently producing a false baseline.

## Scope Boundaries

This packet shrinks the `world-layer` export surface from 10 to 8 and leaves the other three worlds' export surfaces unchanged (they never declared lifecycle exports). It renames `on_print_start` → `from_config` across all four SDK traits, the macro's 15 construction sites, 20 core modules, 9 test guests, and ~30 `slicer-runtime` test files; it deletes `on_print_end` everywhere; it corrects every doc the deletion falsifies, including `docs/03:559-561`, on the rule that the packet deleting a thing deletes its docs. It also makes a stale `pnp_cli` fail loudly at all three spawn sites. It does not introduce per-stage WIT packages, does not touch `SUPPORTED_WIT_WORLDS`/`wit-world`/`validate_wit_world` (packet #3), does not restructure `ExportBinding` into package+interface form (packet #2), and does not extract the three duplicated `pnp_cli` lookups into a shared helper (deferred; needs its own ADR).

## Prerequisites and Blockers

- Depends on: none. First packet of the ADR-0045 queue (`docs/specs/adr-0045-per-stage-wit-packages-plan.md` §Packet Queue).
- Unblocks: `163_per-stage-wit-packages-pilot` (TASK-146b), `164_per-stage-wit-packages-bulk` (TASK-146c). Both consume an 8-export `world-layer` and a lifecycle-free `SlicerModuleSchema.exports`.
- Activation blockers: none. One `[FWD]` remains in `design.md` — extracting the three duplicated `pnp_cli` lookups into a shared helper, deliberately deferred to its own ADR-bearing packet. It is a follow-up, not a blocker: this packet fixes all three sites in place. **The docs/03 and three-spawn-sites `[FWD]`s from earlier drafts are CLOSED — both are in scope here. Do not defer either.**
