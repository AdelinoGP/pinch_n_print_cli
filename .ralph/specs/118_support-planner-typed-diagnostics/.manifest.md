- Step 2 → Step 3 → Step 4 → Step 5 → Step 6 → Step 7: each step's reads include prior step's edits (sequential chain through WIT/SDK/host/audit/guest/tests/planner)

They can theoretically parallel, but Step 7 depends on Step 6's round-trip being green first (Step 7 verification re-runs the planner against the now-proven host path), and Step 6 reads the integration fixture's support-geometry config pattern that Step 7 doesn't touch. **Serialize Steps 6 then 7** to keep the per-step verification chain clean.
- Step 8 reads docs/02/03/05 only; safe to run after Step 7 in parallel with Step 9 prep.
- Step 9 is read-only/verification; final.

**Worker ceiling: 2.** Max safe concurrency is Step 1 (inventory) || nothing else. After inventory, the chain is sequential because each step writes files the next step reads. No parallel write surface exists.

**`workers` parameter: 2** (default; ceiling check passes — but the actual concurrency in this packet is mostly 1 sequential chain; Step 1 may parallel with Step 2 reads, but Step 2's reads are bounded file ranges and Step 1's inventory feeds Step 2 only via manifest, not direct re-read. So W1=Step 1 inventory (read-only) || W2=Step 2 WIT edits (M, may-edit) is the first parallel window.
